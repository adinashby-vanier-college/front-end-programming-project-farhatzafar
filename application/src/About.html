About Me

Biologist

I am a Master’s (MSc) graduate of the Applied Synthetic Biology (SynBioApps) program from the Center for Applied Synthetic Biology (CASB) at Concordia University. My research focused on the cholesterol biosynthesis pathway, specifically the human gene MVK (mevalonate kinase) and its yeast ortholog ERG12. My goal was to create a system that could knockout the yeast gene and replace it with variants of the human gene. This is known as a “humanization” study, in which human genes are engineered into yeast. I succeeded in genetically engineering such a system and using it to perform complementation assays. After completing my Master’s degree during the pandemic, I began a PhD that focused on expanding on my previous research. During the first year of my PhD, I created a mutant library, consisting of over 49 000 unique variants. The variants were tiled, indexed and sequenced using next- generation Illumina sequencing.
